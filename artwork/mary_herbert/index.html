Mary Herbert's work is an intuitive exploration of memory and sensation. She blends painting, drawing, and printmaking to create subtle and mysterious works that hover between the recognized and the unknown. Her current work is a series of small, dreamlike crayon drawings that feel unconscious, surreal, and fantastical. Dreams, memories, and streams of consciousness are recurring themes. She seeks to find her own balance between materiality and the unconscious. Her paintings are filled with a serene beauty that becomes more apparent the longer you look, conveying a sense of gentleness and poetry.
